Distinct Character

No columns, no splits, no cards — just full-width horizontal bands stacking in a disciplined rhythm. White → light grey → dark → white → tinted. The alternation creates pace without any structural complexity.

Accent — Deep Navy

#1e3a5f. The colour of engineering blueprints and technical drawings. Institutional, serious, trustworthy. The navy bottom-border on the nav acts like an engineering baseline — everything is measured from that line.

Service Strip

Four equal-width cards on a cool grey background bridge the white hero and dark stats band. They act as a visual transition zone — the eye steps gradually from light to dark without a harsh cut.

Maximum Simplicity

The most restrained of all four candidates. No decorative elements, no gradients on the hero, no structural tricks. The design's authority comes entirely from proportion, spacing, and the weight of the navy accent.

Distinct Character

Every section is a self-contained card on a dark canvas. The page reads like a project dashboard or architectural site plan — each element occupies its own defined space with pixel-gap borders between them.

Grid Logic

Hero spans two columns, stats fill the third as a vertical stack. Services, partners, and projects each get their own full-width row within the grid. The 1px gap between cards creates structure without dividers.

Accent — Copper

#c17f4e sits between bronze and terracotta — warm but not antique. It reads as material and architectural. The copper gradient line on the hero card is the only decorative element — everything else is geometry.

Dark Canvas

Unlike Candidate 1 where the hero is dark and the rest alternates, here everything lives on the dark canvas. The consistency makes it feel like one unified system rather than sections competing for attention.

Distinct Character

The company name IS the hero — 7rem Montserrat 900 fills the viewport. No hero "section" with background colours — the type itself creates the architectural presence. The outline "HOUSE" reads like a blueprint wireframe.

Gallery Pacing

Projects stack vertically as full-width blocks instead of a grid. Each project gets its own horizontal band — like walking through rooms in a gallery. The eye moves down, not across. Completely different rhythm from the other candidates.

Numbered Services

Services use Space Mono numbering (01, 02, 03, 04) instead of icon cards. The numbers are oversized and ghosted, creating a typographic layer behind the content. Less "UI component", more editorial.

Light Throughout

The only dark element is the thin stats strip. Everything else lives on the warm #f8f6f2 canvas. This is the only candidate where light dominates so completely — a deliberate contrast to the dark-heavy existing options.

Distinct Character

The nav isn't horizontal — it's a permanent dark sidebar. This fundamentally changes how the page reads. The content area feels like opening a folder in an app — structured, contained, purposeful. No other candidate does this.

Warm Canvas

#f5f0e8 — not white, not grey, but warm parchment. The sidebar's ink black against this warmth creates a strong contrast without the industrial coldness of pure white. The green accent connects to nature and sustainability.

Stats as Header

Stats sit at the very top of the content area as a thin dark ticker bar — the first thing you see after the sidebar. This frames all content below as credentials-backed. In other candidates, stats come after the hero.

Vertical Navigation

Links stack vertically with thin separators. CTAs sit at the bottom of the sidebar as stacked buttons. This mirrors how architects present project menus — categorical, hierarchical, permanent.

Card DNA, Scaled Up

The business card's back has angular geometric colour blocks (blue/red/orange triangles). This design takes that exact visual language and makes it the hero's defining element — a diagonal clip-path cuts vivid colour across the white canvas.

Bright, Not Dark

Unlike most other candidates, the hero is white with colour coming to it, not colour sitting on dark. The red stats band is the boldest element on the page — white stat numbers on vivid red. This is the most energetic of all seven candidates.

Colour Roles

Each service card gets a different-coloured top band (red, blue, orange, yellow). Each project card has a gradient tinted to a brand colour. Partner icons use blue and orange. The four colours are everywhere but disciplined — each has a consistent role.

Charcoal Nav

The nav stays charcoal to match the card front, grounding the bright page in the brand's dark identity. The multi-colour stripe under the nav is the direct bridge between the card's stripe motif and the web design.
